Daily Acts of Compassion with no ROI

Daily compassion exists in places where it doesn’t matter all that much. To clarify it’s not that it doesn’t matter at all, it’s just that it doesn’t result in any financial value, material value, or any social capital. Nothing deemed valuable in our society is gained from it. So why do we do it? Why do we give up our seat on the bus? Why do we feel guilty when we don’t give up our seat? In a space where everyday strangers gather and exist in one place for indeterminate amounts of time and disperse at the same rate – we tap someone on the shoulder, gesture to our seat, and get up without exchanging any words? I can’t think of many other places where this type of interaction happens. An act of compassion committed without any possible return other than a nod of appreciation or verbal appreciation.

Empathy keeps our society in check. Empathy makes us give a shit about each other. Empathy is our saving grace when dealt with ignorance, arrogance, and power. Empathy may not always win – but it gives hope, and some days that’s enough.
